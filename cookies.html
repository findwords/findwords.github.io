Cookies Policy

Last Updated: July 2026

This is the Cookies Policy for Find Words, accessible from findwords.github.io. To optimize runtime interaction speeds and maintain UI state accuracy, we leverage browser parameters and local digital storage structures.

1. What Are Cookies and Local Storage?

As is common practice with almost all professional websites, this site uses cookies or modern browser local storage partitions, which are tiny strings downloaded to your computer, to improve your experience. This page describes what information they gather, how we use it and why we sometimes need to store these parameters.

2. How We Use Stored Items

We use local browser items for a variety of reasons detailed below. Unfortunately, in most cases, there are no industry standard options for disabling these without completely disabling the functionality and features they add to this site. It is recommended that you leave on all storage settings if you are not sure whether you need them or not in case they are used to provide a service that you use.

3. The Cookies and Settings We Stored

  • Application Preferences State: When you modify tool metrics like word lengths or toggle difficulty profiles, your state options are committed to browser storage variables so you don't have to redefine them upon a tab refresh cycle.
  • User Configuration State: Your audio settings (e.g., muting sound effects) and your cumulative saved words list are saved locally to ensure continuity between user sessions.

4. Disabling Storage Controls

You can prevent the configuration of storage flags by adjusting the technical settings on your browser (see your browser Help for how to do this). Be aware that disabling cookies or storage components will impact the functionality of this and many other websites that you visit. Disabling these components will usually result in also disabling certain functionality and features of the Find Words program tool suite.

5. Third-Party Web Cookies

In some special cases, we also use content layers or analysis scripts supplied by trusted third parties. Third-party performance measurement systems may follow user browser paths to quantify platform traction metrics and generate reports, empowering us to build richer tool structures for the user base.
